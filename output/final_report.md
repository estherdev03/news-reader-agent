### Ceasefire Takes Effect Between Thailand and Cambodia After Near Three Weeks of Deadly Border Clashes  
After nearly three weeks of fierce fighting displacing around one million civilians, Thailand and Cambodia implemented a ceasefire on 27 December 2025 aimed at halting hostilities along their disputed border. The two defence ministers agreed to freeze front-line positions, ban troop reinforcements, facilitate civilian returns, and remove landmines. Critically, the deal includes the release of 18 Cambodian soldiers held by Thailand since July, expected after 72 hours of ceasefire compliance. The breakthrough followed intensive diplomatic engagement from China and the US, amid longstanding distrust worsened by previous truce failures. Thailand’s Defence Minister cautioned that the ceasefire is a test of Cambodia's sincerity, reserving the right to self-defence if violated. Both sides have suffered losses, with Cambodia notably compelled to retreat from certain positions following Thai air strikes, escalating nationalist grievances. The fragile peace depends on political will overcoming historical grievances and nationalist pressures, with international bodies urging good faith to pave the way for lasting stability.
